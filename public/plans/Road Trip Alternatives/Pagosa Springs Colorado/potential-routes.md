# Potential Routes - Houston to Pagosa Springs, Colorado

[🗺️ View Route on Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/dir/?api=1&origin=1912+West+Dallas+Street%2C+Houston%2C+TX+77019&destination=Pagosa+Springs%2C+CO&waypoints=Amarillo%2C+TX)

## Route Overview
- **Total Distance**: ~900 miles
- **Total Drive Time**: ~13-14 hours (without stops)
- **Strategy**: Break up into 2 days each direction

## Primary Route (Recommended)

### Houston → Pagosa Springs via I-10 W, I-40 W, US-285 S, US-160 W

#### Detailed Route
1. **Houston to Amarillo**: ~600 miles, ~9 hours
   - I-10 W → I-35 N → US-87 N → I-40 W
   - Stopover location (Palo Duro Canyon)

2. **Amarillo to Santa Fe**: ~300 miles, ~4.5 hours
   - I-40 W → US-285 S
   - Enter New Mexico
   - Elevation increases

3. **Santa Fe to Pagosa Springs**: ~200 miles, ~3.5 hours
   - US-285 S → US-160 W
   - Enter Colorado
   - Mountain driving begins
   - **Note**: US-160 through mountains can be challenging in winter

#### Advantages
- Most direct route
- Good stopover point (Amarillo/Palo Duro Canyon or Santa Fe area)
- Generally good road conditions (interstates until final segment)
- Closer than Durango (~200 miles less)

## Recommended Strategy

### Going: Houston → Amarillo → Pagosa Springs
- **Day 1**: Houston to Amarillo/Palo Duro Canyon (~9 hours)
  - Stop: Palo Duro Canyon State Park
  - Stay: Park cabin or nearby nature property
- **Day 2**: Amarillo to Pagosa Springs (~8 hours)
  - Check US-160 conditions before departure
  - Drive carefully in mountain segment
  - Arrive Pagosa Springs, check in

## Weather Considerations

### Texas (Houston to Amarillo)
- **December/January**: Mild to cold
- **Snow**: Rare in Houston/Austin, possible in Panhandle
- **Roads**: Generally clear, well-maintained
- **Concern**: Low

### Colorado/New Mexico (Amarillo to Pagosa Springs)
- **December/January**: Cold, likely snow
- **Elevation**: Increases significantly
- **Roads**: US-160 can be challenging in winter
- **Concern**: Moderate to high - check CDOT conditions
