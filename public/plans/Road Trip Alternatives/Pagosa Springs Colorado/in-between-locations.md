# In-Between Locations to Stay Overnight - Pagosa Springs, Colorado

## Requirements
- **One stop going** (Houston → Pagosa Springs)
- **One stop coming** (Pagosa Springs → Houston)
- **Purpose**: Break up the ~13-14 hour drive
- **Preferences**: Nature/forest settings preferred, kitchen preferred
- **Budget**: Keep costs reasonable (part of overall budget)

## Drive Overview
- **Total Distance**: ~900 miles (Houston to Pagosa Springs)
- **Drive Time**: ~13-14 hours (without stops)
- **Strategy**: Break into 2 days each direction (~6-7 hours driving per day)

## Going: Houston → Pagosa Springs

### Option 1: Northern Route (Recommended for Nature)
**Houston → Amarillo Area → Pagosa Springs**

### [Amarillo, TX Area](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Amarillo%2C%20TX%20Area)
- **Distance from Houston**: ~600 miles (~9 hours)
- **Distance to Pagosa Springs**: ~400 miles (~8 hours)
- **Nature Options**: Palo Duro Canyon State Park, Caprock Canyons State Park
- **Lodging Research Needed**:
  - [ ] Research: Hotels with kitchenettes
  - [ ] Research: Airbnbs near Palo Duro Canyon
  - [ ] Check: Properties in/near state parks

### [Palo Duro Canyon](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Palo%20Duro%20Canyon)
State Park (30 min from Amarillo)
- **Location**: 30 minutes from Amarillo
- **Features**:
  - Camping available (check winter availability)
  - Cabins may be available
  - In-park accommodations
- **Note**: Major nature attraction, perfect stopover location

### [Santa Fe](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Santa%20Fe)
area (Alternative)
- **Distance from Houston**: ~900 miles (~13 hours) - TOO LONG for one day
- **Distance to Pagosa Springs**: ~200 miles (~3.5 hours)
- **Nature Options**: Mountain/forest settings
- **Note**: Would need intermediate stop first

## Coming: Pagosa Springs → Houston

### Option 1: Reverse Northern Route
**Pagosa Springs → Amarillo Area → Houston**

### [Amarillo Area (Return Stopover)](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Amarillo%20Area)
- **Palo Duro Canyon** area again (if enjoyed)
- Or try different property for variety

## Recommended Strategy

### Going: Houston → Amarillo Area → Pagosa Springs
- **Day 1**: Houston to Amarillo/Palo Duro Canyon area (~9 hours)
  - Stay: Palo Duro Canyon State Park or nearby nature property
- **Day 2**: Amarillo area to Pagosa Springs (~8 hours)
  - Check US-160 conditions before departure
  - Drive carefully in mountain segment
  - Arrive Pagosa Springs, check in

### Coming: Pagosa Springs → Amarillo Area → Houston
- **Day 1**: Pagosa Springs to Amarillo/Palo Duro Canyon area (~8 hours)
  - Check US-160 conditions
  - Stay: Same area or different property
- **Day 2**: Amarillo area to Houston (~9 hours)
  - Return home

## Cost Considerations
- **Budget per stopover**: Aim for $50-100/night if possible
- **Total stopover cost**: $100-200 (2 nights total)
- **State park cabins**: Often more affordable than hotels

## Notes
- **Amarillo area** is the most logical halfway point
- **Palo Duro Canyon** offers nature + accommodation in one place
- **Winter conditions**: Verify road access to any remote properties
- **Flexibility**: Have backup options in case primary choice unavailable
- **Kitchen preference**: Cooking at stopovers saves money and time
- **Mountain driving**: US-160 can be challenging - check CDOT conditions
