# Potential Routes - Houston to Taos, New Mexico

[🗺️ View Route on Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/dir/?api=1&origin=1912+West+Dallas+Street%2C+Houston%2C+TX+77019&destination=Taos%2C+NM&waypoints=Amarillo%2C+TX)

## Route Overview
- **Total Distance**: ~900 miles
- **Total Drive Time**: ~13-14 hours (without stops)
- **Strategy**: Break up into 2 days each direction

## Primary Route (Recommended)

### Houston → Taos via I-10 W, I-40 W, US-285 N, US-64 W

#### Detailed Route
1. **Houston to Amarillo**: ~600 miles, ~9 hours
   - I-10 W → I-35 N → US-87 N → I-40 W
   - Stopover location (Palo Duro Canyon)

2. **Amarillo to Santa Fe**: ~300 miles, ~4.5 hours
   - I-40 W → US-285 N
   - Enter New Mexico
   - Elevation increases

3. **Santa Fe to Taos**: ~70 miles, ~1.5 hours
   - US-285 N → US-64 W
   - Mountain driving begins
   - **Note**: US-64 through mountains can be challenging in winter

#### Advantages
- Most direct route
- Good stopover point (Amarillo/Palo Duro Canyon)
- Santa Fe area option for stopover
- Generally good road conditions (interstates until final segment)
- Closer than Durango (~200 miles less)

## Recommended Strategy

### Going: Houston → Amarillo → Taos
- **Day 1**: Houston to Amarillo/Palo Duro Canyon (~9 hours)
  - Stop: Palo Duro Canyon State Park
  - Stay: Park cabin or nearby nature property
- **Day 2**: Amarillo to Taos (~6 hours)
  - Check US-64 conditions before departure
  - Drive carefully in mountain segment
  - Arrive Taos, check in

### Coming: Taos → Amarillo → Houston
- **Day 1**: Taos to Amarillo/Palo Duro Canyon (~6 hours)
  - Check US-64 conditions
  - Stay: Same area or different property
- **Day 2**: Amarillo to Houston (~9 hours)
  - Return home

## Weather Considerations

### Texas (Houston to Amarillo)
- **December/January**: Mild to cold
- **Snow**: Rare in Houston/Austin, possible in Panhandle
- **Roads**: Generally clear, well-maintained
- **Concern**: Low

### New Mexico (Amarillo to Taos)
- **December/January**: Cold, likely snow
- **Elevation**: Increases significantly
- **Roads**: US-64 can be challenging in winter
- **Concern**: Moderate to high - check NM DOT conditions
