# Taos, New Mexico - Trip Overview

![Taos New Mexico](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1511497584788-876760111969?w=800&h=600&fit=crop)

## Destination Summary
- **Distance from Houston**: ~900 miles (~13-14 hours driving)
- **Location**: North of Houston, in the Sangre de Cristo Mountains of New Mexico
- **Primary Attraction**: Taos Ski Valley + Carson National Forest + High Desert Mountains
- **Best For**: Guaranteed snow, mountain forests, shorter than Durango, unique culture

- **Temperatures**: Very cold (10-20°F lows, 34-48°F highs)
- **Precipitation**: Snow likely, especially later in trip
- **Conditions**: Mountain conditions - cold, variable
- **Elevation**: ~6,900 feet at Taos

## Why This Destination?
✅ **Guaranteed Snow**: Very likely to see snow-covered landscapes
✅ **Closer Than Durango**: ~200 miles closer, ~2-3 hours less driving
✅ **Mountain Forests**: Carson National Forest - extensive forested areas
✅ **Unique Culture**: Taos Pueblo, art scene, unique New Mexico culture
✅ **Nature Focus**: National forest, ski area, mountain scenery
⚠️ **Mountain Driving**: Some mountain roads, but less extreme than Durango's US-550

## Key Attractions
- **Taos Ski Valley**: Ski area, guaranteed snow, mountain scenery
- **Carson National Forest**: Extensive mountain forests, hiking
- **Taos Pueblo**: UNESCO World Heritage Site, ancient adobe structures
- **Rio Grande Gorge**: Deep canyon, scenic views
- **Wheeler Peak**: Highest point in New Mexico (13,161 ft)
- **Enchanted Circle Scenic Byway**: 84-mile scenic drive

## Accommodation Notes
- Many properties in forest/mountain settings
- Kitchen facilities commonly available
- Options near Carson National Forest
- Holiday period may have higher rates
- Mix of ski area condos and mountain cabins

## Driving Route
- **Primary Route**: I-10 W → I-35 N → I-40 W → US-285 N → US-64 W
- **Mountain Segment**: US-64 through mountains - check conditions
- **Stopover**: Amarillo or Santa Fe area recommended
- **Winter Hazards**: Mountain roads can be challenging - check NM DOT conditions

## Important Considerations
⚠️ **Mountain Driving**: Some mountain roads, check conditions before trip
⚠️ **Mountain Weather**: Conditions can change quickly
⚠️ **Elevation**: High elevation - prepare for cold temperatures
⚠️ **Road Closures**: Mountain passes can close during severe weather
