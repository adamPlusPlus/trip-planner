### Route 1: Northern Route (Recommended)
**Houston → Austin → Lubbock → Amarillo → Raton → Durango**

#### Overview
- **Total Distance**: ~1,100 miles
- **Total Drive Time**: ~15-16 hours
- **Primary Roads**: I-10 W → I-35 N → US-87 N → I-40 W → US-64 W → US-550 N

#### Detailed Route
1. **Houston to Austin**: ~165 miles, ~2.5 hours
   - I-10 W to I-35 N
   - Nature options: Bastrop State Park area

2. **Austin to Lubbock**: ~380 miles, ~5.5 hours
   - I-35 N to US-84 W
   - Mostly plains/grassland

3. **Lubbock to Amarillo**: ~120 miles, ~2 hours
   - US-87 N
   - Stopover location (Palo Duro Canyon)

4. **Amarillo to Raton, NM**: ~200 miles, ~3 hours
   - I-40 W
   - Cross into New Mexico
   - Elevation increases

5. **Raton to Durango**: ~235 miles, ~3.5 hours
   - US-64 W to US-550 N
   - Mountain driving begins
   - **Note**: US-550 (Million Dollar Highway) can be challenging in winter
   - Check conditions before this segment

#### Advantages
- Most direct route
- Good stopover point (Amarillo/Palo Duro Canyon)
- Palo Duro Canyon is major nature attraction
- Generally good road conditions (interstates)
- Less desert than southern route

#### Disadvantages
- Final segment (US-550) can be challenging in winter
- Some areas are more plains than "green"
- Less dramatic scenery until Colorado

#### Nature Highlights
- Bastrop State Park area (Lost Pines)
- Palo Duro Canyon State Park (major stop)
- Caprock Canyons (alternative)
- Mountain scenery in Colorado

### Route 2: Southern Route (More Desert, Longer)
**Houston → San Antonio → El Paso → Las Cruces → Albuquerque → Durango**

#### Overview
- **Total Distance**: ~1,200+ miles
- **Total Drive Time**: ~18+ hours
- **Primary Roads**: I-10 W → I-25 N → US-550 N

#### Detailed Route
1. **Houston to San Antonio**: ~200 miles, ~3 hours
   - I-10 W

2. **San Antonio to El Paso**: ~550 miles, ~8 hours
   - I-10 W
   - Long desert stretch
   - **Note**: Very long single-day drive

3. **El Paso to Las Cruces**: ~45 miles, ~1 hour
   - I-10 W
   - Enter New Mexico

4. **Las Cruces to Albuquerque**: ~225 miles, ~3.5 hours
   - I-25 N
   - Elevation increases

5. **Albuquerque to Durango**: ~300 miles, ~5 hours
   - I-25 N to US-550 N
   - Mountain driving

#### Advantages
- More varied scenery (desert to mountains)
- Could visit Big Bend area (major detour though)
- Different experience than northern route

#### Disadvantages
- **Significantly longer** (100+ miles, 3+ hours)
- More desert driving (less "green")
- Harder to find good stopover point (distances awkward)
- El Paso to Albuquerque is long stretch
- Less ideal for nature preferences

#### Nature Highlights
- Big Bend area (if major detour taken)
- New Mexico desert landscapes
- Mountain scenery in Colorado

## Recommended: Route 1 (Northern Route)

### Why Route 1?
1. **Most direct**: Shortest distance and time
2. **Best stopover**: Amarillo/Palo Duro Canyon is perfect halfway point
3. **Nature attraction**: Palo Duro Canyon is major natural feature
4. **Better roads**: Mostly interstates until final segment
5. **Fuel efficiency**: Shorter distance = lower fuel costs
6. **Budget friendly**: Less driving = less fuel = more budget for accommodations

### Route 1 Detailed Breakdown

#### Segment 1: Houston to Amarillo (Stopover)
- **Distance**: ~600 miles
- **Time**: ~9 hours
- **Roads**: I-10 W → I-35 N → US-87 N → I-40 W
- **Nature**: Bastrop area, then plains, then Palo Duro Canyon
- **Stopover**: Palo Duro Canyon State Park or nearby

#### Segment 2: Amarillo to Durango
- **Distance**: ~500 miles
- **Time**: ~7 hours
- **Roads**: I-40 W → US-64 W → US-550 N
- **Nature**: Plains transition to mountains
- **Critical**: US-550 (Million Dollar Highway) - check conditions!

### US-550 (Million Dollar Highway) Considerations
- **Route**: From US-64 junction to Durango
- **Conditions**: Can be challenging in winter
- **Snow**: May have snow/ice, especially at higher elevations
- **Chains**: May be required in bad weather
- **Alternatives**: Check if there are alternative routes if conditions are bad
- **Timing**: Try to drive this segment in daylight, good weather
- **Research**: Check CDOT road conditions before this segment

## Alternative Routes if US-550 is Problematic

### If US-550 is too dangerous:
- **Option A**: Take I-25 N from Raton to Colorado Springs, then US-24 W to US-285 S
  - Longer but may be safer in winter
  - More interstate driving

- **Option B**: Check CDOT for recommended winter routes
  - Conditions change daily
  - Have backup plan ready

## Weather Considerations by Route Segment

- **December/January**: Cold, possible snow

### Colorado (Border to Durango)

- **Roads**: US-550 can be challenging
