# Durango, Colorado - Trip Overview

![Durango Colorado](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1511497584788-876760111969?w=800&h=600&fit=crop)

## Destination Summary
- **Distance from Houston**: ~1,100 miles (~15-16 hours driving)
- **Location**: Northwest of Houston, in the San Juan Mountains of Colorado
- **Primary Attraction**: Mesa Verde National Park + San Juan National Forest + Mountain Scenery
- **Best For**: Guaranteed snow, mountain forests, national parks, unique biomes

### Snow Expectations
- **Snow Likelihood**: High (guaranteed snow viewing)
- **Typical Snow**: Moderate snowfall likely, especially Jan 2-3
- **Snow on Ground**: Very likely, snow-covered landscapes expected
- **Driving Impact**: Moderate to high - US-550 (Million Dollar Highway) can be challenging

### Weather Summary
- **Temperatures**: Cold (21-31°F lows, 42-54°F highs)
- **Precipitation**: Snow likely, especially later in trip
- **Conditions**: Mountain conditions - variable, can change quickly
- **Elevation**: ~6,500 feet at Durango

## Why This Destination?
✅ **Guaranteed Snow**: Very likely to see snow-covered landscapes
✅ **National Parks**: Mesa Verde National Park nearby
✅ **Mountain Forests**: San Juan National Forest - extensive forested areas
✅ **Unique Biomes**: Desert to mountains transition, unique ecosystems
✅ **Nature Focus**: Multiple national parks, forests, scenic drives
⚠️ **Driving Challenge**: US-550 (Million Dollar Highway) requires careful planning

## Key Attractions
- **Mesa Verde National Park**: Ancient cliff dwellings, unique history
- **San Juan National Forest**: Extensive mountain forests, hiking
- **Durango & Silverton Narrow Gauge Railroad**: Historic train through mountains
- **Animas River Trail**: Scenic river trail
- **Purgatory Resort Area**: Ski area, mountain scenery
- **Million Dollar Highway**: Scenic but challenging mountain drive
- **San Juan Skyway**: Scenic mountain byway

## Accommodation Notes
- Many properties in forest/mountain settings
- Kitchen facilities commonly available
- Options near San Juan National Forest
- Holiday period may have higher rates
- Some properties may require 4WD for access

## Driving Route
- **Primary Route**: I-10 W → I-35 N → US-87 N → I-40 W → US-64 W → US-550 N
- **Critical Segment**: US-550 (Million Dollar Highway) - check conditions before driving
- **Stopover**: Amarillo/Palo Duro Canyon area recommended
- **Winter Hazards**: Mountain passes can be challenging - check CDOT road conditions

## Important Considerations
⚠️ **US-550 Driving**: Research conditions before trip, may need alternative route
⚠️ **Mountain Weather**: Conditions can change quickly
⚠️ **Elevation**: High elevation - prepare for cold temperatures
⚠️ **Road Closures**: Mountain passes can close during severe weather
