# Things to See - Durango, Colorado


## Route Planning Notes
- Focus: National parks, state parks, unique biomes
- Avoid: Urban areas
- Preference: Green areas, but desert acceptable for driving if not too much diversion

## Texas Attractions (East to West)

### [Bastrop State Park](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Bastrop%20State%20Park)
- **Location**: Lost Pines region, ~2.5 hours from Houston
- **Features**:
  - Unique pine forest ecosystem
  - Hiking trails, camping
- **Note**: Beautiful pine forest setting

### [McKinney Roughs Nature Park](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=McKinney%20Roughs%20Nature%20Park)
- **Location**: Near Bastrop
- **Features**:
  - Diverse ecosystems
  - Hiking, horseback riding trails
  - Educational programs

### [Palo Duro Canyon State Park](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Palo%20Duro%20Canyon%20State%20Park)
- **Location**: Near Amarillo
- **Features**:
  - "Grand Canyon of Texas"
  - Second-largest canyon in the U.S.
  - Dramatic geological formations
  - Hiking trails, scenic drives
- **Note**: Good stopover location (halfway point)

### [Caprock Canyons State Park](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Caprock%20Canyons%20State%20Park)
- **Location**: Near Quitaque, TX
- **Features**:
  - Red rock canyons
  - Bison herd
  - Hiking trails
- **Note**: Alternative to Palo Duro if time allows

### [Big Bend Ranch State Park](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Big%20Bend%20Ranch%20State%20Park)
- **Location**: Far West Texas (if taking southern route)
- **Features**:
  - Largest state park in Texas
  - Rugged desert landscapes
  - Rio Grande views
- **Note**: Significant detour from direct route

### [Guadalupe Mountains National Park](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Guadalupe%20Mountains%20National%20Park)
- **Location**: Far West Texas (if taking southern route)
- **Features**:
  - Highest peak in Texas
  - Desert/mountain biome
  - Hiking opportunities
- **Note**: Check accessibility in winter

## New Mexico Attractions

### [Bandelier National Monument](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Bandelier%20National%20Monument)
- **Location**: Near Los Alamos
- **Features**:
  - Ancient Pueblo cliff dwellings
  - Forested canyons
  - Hiking trails
- **Note**: May have snow/ice in winter

### [Valles Caldera National Preserve](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Valles%20Caldera%20National%20Preserve)
- **Features**:
  - Volcanic caldera
  - Unique high-altitude grassland ecosystem
  - Wildlife viewing
- **Note**: Check winter access

### [Kasha-Katuwe Tent Rocks National Monument](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Kasha-Katuwe%20Tent%20Rocks%20National%20Monument)
- **Features**:
  - Unique rock formations
  - Slot canyon hiking
- **Note**: Check winter accessibility

## Colorado Attractions (Near Durango)

### [Mesa Verde National Park](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Mesa%20Verde%20National%20Park)
- **Location**: 1 hour from Durango
- **Features**:
  - Ancient cliff dwellings
  - Archaeological sites
  - Scenic drives
- **Note**: Some areas may be closed in winter, check ahead

### [San Juan National Forest](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=San%20Juan%20National%20Forest)
- **Location**: Multiple access points near Durango
- **Features**:
  - Extensive forested areas
  - Hiking trails
  - Scenic drives

### Scenic Drives Near Durango
- **Million Dollar Highway** (US 550 to Silverton/Ouray)
  - Stunning mountain scenery
  - Note: Can be challenging in winter, check conditions
  - Snow chains may be required

- **San Juan Skyway** (scenic byway)
  - Mountain passes
  - Forested areas
  - Note: Winter driving conditions vary

## Unique Biomes to Experience
1. **Lost Pines** (Bastrop area) - Unique pine ecosystem
2. **High Plains** (Texas Panhandle) - Grassland biome
3. **Desert** (West Texas/New Mexico) - If taking southern route
4. **High Desert** (New Mexico) - Unique elevation-based desert
5. **Mountain Forest** (Colorado) - Alpine/subalpine forests
6. **Snow-covered landscapes** (Durango area) - Winter biome

## Planning Considerations
- **Winter Access**: Many high-elevation areas may have limited access or require 4WD
- **Park Hours**: Some parks have reduced hours in winter
- **Road Conditions**: Check CDOT (Colorado) and state DOT websites for conditions
- **Entrance Fees**: Budget for park entrance fees ($10-30 per park typically)
- **Time Allocation**: Balance driving time with exploration time

## Recommended Priority Stops
1. **Palo Duro Canyon** (if taking northern route) - Major natural attraction, good halfway point
2. **Mesa Verde National Park** - World-class archaeological site near Durango
3. **San Juan National Forest** - Extensive nature access near Durango
4. **Scenic mountain drives** - If weather permits safely
